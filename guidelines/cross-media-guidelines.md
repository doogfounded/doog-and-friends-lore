# Cross Media Guidelines
One of the main priorities when making this story was to make it work across different mediums or 
in software terms make it portable across different platforms. Here are some guidelines to follow for when writing.

1. Write actions, not just thoughts

   Stories must be understandable without relying on internal monologues. Try to actually show what a character is doing
   not just what they are thinking.

2. Every scene must have a clear objective

   All scenes must answer two questions. What does the character want right now? What's stopping them?

3. Avoid long exposition

   Prioritize having conflicts over explanation. Info should come from arguments, tension, and consqeuences.

4. Make characters functionally distinct

   Each character should react differently under pressure and have different goals and motivations while having a clear role in
   the system of the story.

5. Make scenes that can be visualized

   Avoid scenes that are dependant on narration, overly abstract, or purely internal.

6. Dialogue must be performable

   Dialogue should sound natural when spoken out loud, avoid over explaining, and carry subtext whenever possible.

7. Make all parts modular and flexible

   Write scenes and ideas so that they can be easily moved, expanded, or cut out entirely. Avoid having things be dependant
   on one sequence.

8. Respect canon Consistency

   Before submitting a pull request, check existing character behavior and check the timeline and relationships. Major changes must have major explainations.

9. Leave room for expansion

    Do not over define every single little thing or detail. Just try to suggest details instead of locking them all down to allow for future
    contributions to your idea.

10. Focus on Consequences

    At the core, the meaning or themes of this story are about choices, outcomes, and escalation. If you pull request doesn't change
    anything about the story, it's most likely not needed.

Bonus Tip:

Try to optimize as much as you can for the specific medium as well while keeping the core story of Doog and Friends and while still using these guidelines to keep consistency so at the very least it feels correct. It sounds contradictory at first but if software devs can work around limits and give you the same experience then this should be possible.
