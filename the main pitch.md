Doog is a small-time crook who somehow finds himself trying to lead and
grow his crew into something bigger—something close to an “empire.”

As he and his friends get older, he struggles with the increasing
responsibilities of their lives and the weight of what they’re building.
Doog has to rely on logic, reasoning, and strategy to keep his crew
together, while also attempting compassion, encouragement, and
motivation—efforts that often come out awkward or misread.

Conflict is constant. Arguments, noise, and clashing personalities
follow the group everywhere. Whether they’re in the middle of a deal,
planning a heist, or just playing games during downtime, Doog has to
keep his composure, guide them forward, and come up with a plan—all
while managing his own anger.

Despite the chaos, success does happen. Things don’t always go smoothly,
and everyone often operates in their own headspace, but through a mix of
Doog’s messy determination, resilience, and sheer luck, situations
somehow come together. One plan might fall apart, only for another to
unexpectedly pull things back in the crew’s favor.

At times, Doog’s life feels almost random—like things are just barely
holding together, yet still working out. That unpredictability becomes
part of how he and his crew function as a team.

He’s ambitious, often daydreaming about a bigger life. But as things
escalate, he begins to recognize the damage being done. Trying to stop
or change direction proves far more difficult than he expected.

Doog’s story is loud, messy, tense, and at times darkly comedic—inviting
the audience to watch, question, and reflect on what it all really
means.
